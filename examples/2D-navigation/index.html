Overview/Motivation

Intrinsic Alignment (IA)

Intrinsic Alignment (IA) is the tendency of galaxies to align with their neighboring galaxies and the underlying large scale structure. This effect can masquerade as a weak gravitational lensing signal and contribute to the systematic errors of upcoming weak lensing surveys.

Image credit: Troxel & Ishak

  • Analytic Models in the Literature
    • NLA from Hirata & Seljak, TATT from Blazek et al 2016
    • Small scale Halo models

    Intrinsic Alignment of Bulges and Discs (complete)

    Science goal: How do dynamically decomposed/classified galaxies exhibit IA

    • Measure IA signal using the analytic NLA model on large scales (> 4 Mpc)

    • We see a clear decreasing IA signal with increasing disky-ness
    • Bulges do show statistically consistent signal with the Ellipticals
    • Morphologically classified/decomposed samples reveal a much more complex
      picture compared to the traditional color split samples
